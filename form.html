To stay in the UK after this date, you will need to have either pre-settled status or settled status under the EU Settlement Scheme (or another type of permission to stay).

Prove your Status

If you need to prove your immigration status to someone, you can do this online with a share code.

What you can do in the UK

Your pre-settled status means you can:

  • live in the UK
  • work
  • study
  • rent a place to live
  • use the National Health Service (NHS) in a similar way to a permanent UK resident
  • access public funds if eligible
  • access a current account with a bank or building society in the UK
  • travel in and out of the UK. You may need required documents

Legal basis of status

This status was granted in accordance with the EU exit separation agreements:

  • for EU citizens, Withdrawal Agreement
  • for EEA EFTA citizens, EEA EFTA Separation Agreement
  • for Swiss citizens, Swiss Citizens' Rights Agreement

Keep your details up to date

You must keep your personal details up to date in your UKVI account.

You can check and update the details in your UKVI account including your:

  • phone number
  • email address
  • home address
  • Passport or identity documents

If there is an error on your eVisa

If any of the information is wrong, you can report an error with your eVisa

Before you travel

You may be delayed or denied boarding if you have not added your passport or identity document to your account.

Use the update your UKVI account details service to add a passport or identity document.

Nationality Information

When you add a passport or identity document to your account, the nationality will be displayed on your eVisa.
